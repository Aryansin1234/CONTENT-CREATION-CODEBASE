FIRST LINE (125 characters max — shown before "more")
  This is the only part 90% of people read.
  Must be: punchy, specific, and make someone want to tap "more"

  Formats that work:
  → "This one [thing] saves [X] hours every week"
  → "No one talks about [specific fact] and it's costing teams [specific consequence]"
  → "[Number] things I wish I knew before [specific decision]"
  → "[Tool/concept] explained in 30 seconds"

  Avoid:
  → Generic: "AI is amazing and here's why"
  → Clickbait with no substance: "You won't believe this tool"
  → Starting with a hashtag

CAPTION BODY (150–200 characters of actual content, after first line)
  1–3 sentences max. The payoff for tapping "more".
  Be specific about what they get from the image/carousel/post.
  End with a soft CTA: "Save this", "Which would you choose?", "Tag someone who needs this"

  Then a blank line before hashtags.

HASHTAGS (25–30 tags on a new line after the blank line)
  Structure: 5 broad + 10 medium niche + 10 very specific

  Broad (5):        #tech #programming #developer #coding #software
  Medium niche (10): based on the technology category — e.g. #javascript #reactjs #nodejs
  Very specific (10): the specific tools/concepts in the post — e.g. #langchain #langgraph #llmops

  Mix capitalisation for readability: #JavaScript not always #javascript
  No spaces in multi-word tags: #machineLearning not #machine learning
  Avoid banned/shadowbanned tags. Avoid generic vanity tags like #success #hustle.

EMOJIS (3–5 used naturally in the text)
  Use to: break up text visually, add energy, signal categories
  Place within the sentence, not at the end of every line.
  Match the tone — no party emojis for serious technical content.
  Good: "Build a full RAG pipeline ⚡ in under 50 lines of Python"
  Bad: "This is so cool 🔥🔥🔥 check it out! 🚀✨💯"
