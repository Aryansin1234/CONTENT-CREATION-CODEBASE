━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

"tutorial"
  The article explains HOW to do something step-by-step. It has actionable instructions,
  code examples, or a clear process a reader can follow. The reader leaves knowing how
  to do something they couldn't before.
  Examples: "How to build a RAG pipeline", "Setting up Redis with Docker in 10 minutes"

"news"
  A factual announcement, release, or event that just happened or is happening now.
  Focus is on WHAT happened and WHY it matters, not on instructions. Time-sensitive.
  Examples: "OpenAI launches GPT-5", "GitHub acquires npm", "React 20 released"

"opinion"
  A perspective, argument, or hot take on a topic in tech. The article takes a stance
  that can be agreed or disagreed with. It argues FOR or AGAINST something.
  Examples: "TypeScript is overrated", "The problem with microservices", "AI won't replace developers"

"tool_spotlight"
  A product, library, framework, or service being highlighted or launched. Focus is on
  WHAT the tool does, the problem it solves, and who it's for. Not a tutorial — it doesn't
  teach you to use it deeply, just showcases it.
  Examples: "Introducing Bun 2.0", "shadcn/ui is changing how we build", "Cursor vs Copilot compared"

"deep_dive"
  A long-form, technically detailed exploration of a concept, system, or architecture.
  Goes deeper than a tutorial. Covers internals, tradeoffs, design decisions. Readers
  are left with a thorough mental model.
  Examples: "How LLM tokenisation actually works", "Inside Postgres MVCC", "The architecture of Discord's message queue"
