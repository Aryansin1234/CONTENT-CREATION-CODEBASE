FORMAT REQUIREMENTS

Length          800–1,200 characters (including spaces). Count carefully.
Paragraphs      2–4 paragraphs separated by a single blank line
Emojis          Maximum 3. Use only when they add meaning, not decoration.
                Good: 🧵 to signal a list, ⚠️ to signal a warning
                Bad: 🚀 at the end of every line
Hashtags        3–5 at the very end on their own line. Lowercase, relevant, not generic.
                Bad: #tech #ai #innovation
                Good: #llmops #langchain #agentic

OPENING LINE (make or break)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The first line must stop the scroll. It shows in the feed before "see more".
Approaches that work (pick one based on content type):

→ THE COUNTERINTUITIVE FACT
  State something specific that contradicts common assumption
  "Most RAG pipelines fail not because of the LLM — it's the chunking."

→ THE SPECIFIC NUMBER
  Lead with data or a concrete metric
  "I spent 3 months debugging a 'simple' async bug. The fix was 2 lines."

→ THE DIRECT STATEMENT
  Say the thing people are thinking but not saying
  "Your microservices architecture has nothing to do with Netflix."

→ THE PATTERN OBSERVATION
  "Every time I see a team stuck on deployment, it's the same 3 things."

NEVER start with:
  - "I" as the first word
  - "Excited to share"
  - "I'm thrilled"
  - "It's been a journey"
  - A generic statement about the topic (e.g. "AI is changing everything")

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
BODY STRUCTURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Paragraph 1 (after the hook):
  Expand on the hook with context. Why does this matter? What's the situation
  that makes this relevant? Keep it to 2–3 sentences. Stay specific.

Paragraph 2:
  The substance. Cover the 2–3 most important key points.
  Use short sentences. One idea per sentence.
  Avoid: long compound sentences, passive voice, hedge words ("could", "might", "arguably")

Paragraph 3 (optional):
  The implication or what to do with this. One sentence if needed.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
CLOSING QUESTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

End with a question that:
  - Has a definitive answer people want to share (not a vague "what do you think?")
  - Is specific enough that a developer or tech manager can answer from their own experience
  - Creates genuine debate or invites people to share a pattern they've seen

Bad:  "What do you think about AI?"
Bad:  "Have you tried this? Let me know in the comments!"
Good: "Have you found a better way to handle this in production?"
Good: "What's the thing you wish someone had told you before starting this?"

THINGS TO AVOID
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

❌ "Game-changing", "revolutionary", "groundbreaking", "powerful", "robust", "seamless"
❌ Passive voice: "it has been found", "this can be used to"
❌ Filler openings: "In today's fast-paced world...", "As we know..."
❌ Bullet point lists inside the post (use prose — this is not a slide deck)
❌ Mentioning the original article title or source
❌ Summarising the article — add your perspective, don't just report
